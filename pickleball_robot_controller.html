Readable wiring and build book · Rev R1 · companion to the KiCad PCB source

Use this PDF to understand and wire the robot. The KiCad schematic remains the authoritative PCB/netlist file; this version deliberately splits the system into readable pages.

1. Power architecture

PI5 V / 5 A

U4 powers only the Raspberry Pi 5 and Hailo connection. It is a supply capability, not constant draw.

LOGIC5 V control rail

U1 powers ESP32 carrier and low-current logic. Never use it for the Pi or servos.

SERVOS6 V / 5 A

U10 supplies the three arm servos. Avoid deliberately stalling multiple servos at once.

4. Three-degree-of-freedom arm servos

What plugs where

HeaderServoPCA9685 channel
J12Main 25 kg swing servo0
J13MG996R elbow servo1
J14MG90S wrist servo2

Safe first test

  1. Power the board from a current-limited bench supply.
  2. Check the amber LED and measure 6.0 V at a servo header.
  3. Connect one servo only, with arm free to move.
  4. Use slow movements and PWM limits before connecting all three.

Page 4 of 5 · Servo headers are ordinary 2.54 mm male pins, not JST-XH.

5. Build and PCB-layout checklist

Easy normal-iron parts

  • All 0805 resistors and small ceramic capacitors
  • Through-hole 220 µF / 1000 µF / 2200 µF electrolytic capacitors
  • JST-PH motor headers, Pi connector, XT60 pigtail connector, servo headers
  • Fuses and large terminal parts

Heat gun / paste parts

  • U2/U3 VNH5019 motor drivers
  • U4/U10 TPS54560B buck regulators
  • U5 PCA9685 and large power inductors

The exact manufacturer part numbers are in ORDERING_GUIDE.md.

Do before ordering

  1. Open the KiCad project and assign/verify every footprint.
  2. Place the high-current parts first; use wide copper pours and thermal vias.
  3. Keep buck switch nodes short and away from encoder/UART traces.
  4. Keep ESP32 antenna area clear of copper and components.
  5. Run ERC, then update PCB from schematic and run DRC.
  6. Print J4/J5 at 1:1 scale and compare with your physical motor plug.
Do not power the Pi or servos from the ESP32 logic rail. Each has its own regulator on the same 3S battery.
